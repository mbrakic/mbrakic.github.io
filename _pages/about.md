## Who am I?

I started my PhD in the CMTH group (Condensed Matter THeory) at Imperial
College in 2020, finishing in 2024. During my time there, I worked on advancing
supersolid theory (see a summary of my work here). Supersolids are interesting
and valuable for two reasons primarily:
* They're relatively poorly understood and a new phase of matter. This is
  usually enough by itself to ask questions.
* Superfluids (and their cooler younger cousins supersolids) are closely related
  to *superconductors*, which are even less understood and of drastically more
  relevance to the world at large.
My work bridged a gap between microscopic and macroscopic descriptions of
supersolids, showing how peculiar bulk behaviours arise purely from non-local
momentum space interactions.

Whilst I was studying for my PhD, I'd had a passing interest in machine
learning, though admittedly that interest became considerably amplified
following the rapid developments in LLMs I'd seen over that period. This
emerging (to me, though it'd been in development for many years) field was
exciting, powerful, and also a bit concerning. Sure, I had some worries about
superintelligence and the AGI etc. etc., but I felt that the misuse of these
tools, either by bad actors or by incompetent users, was considerably more
pressing. 

Though I felt like there was important and valuable efforts to be made in
supersolids and adjacent fields, I ultimately felt that the world offered more
opportunity to do meaningful and impactful work in ML. This *prompted* me to
transition into the AI safety sector, where I've since been working as a postdoc
in SAIL (Safe Artificial Intelligence Lab) with the wonderful people there as
part of Alessio Lomuscio's group. My work currently focuses on PAC (Probably
Approximately Correct) bounds for Lipschitz constants in neural networks, which
you can read more about here. 

In terms of being a human person, my interests are primarily in reading, playing table
tennis, good food, good company and knowing a little about a lot of things
(trivia tapas).
